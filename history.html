Scottish Castles

Scotland is home to over two thousand castles, many of which date back to the Middle Ages. These castles were built for defense, residence, and to show the power of noble families.

Famous castles such as Edinburgh Castle and Stirling Castle played key roles in protecting the kingdom and controlling strategic locations.

Clans and Society

Scottish society was traditionally organized into clans. Each clan was led by a chief and united by family ties, loyalty, and shared land.

Clans often wore specific tartan patterns to represent their identity and heritage. These traditions remain important today.

Wars and Battles

Scotland fought many wars to defend its independence. The Wars of Independence in the 13th and 14th centuries were especially important.

Heroes like William Wallace and Robert the Bruce became symbols of resistance, courage, and freedom.

Kings and Monarchy

Scotland was ruled by its own kings for centuries. These rulers shaped laws, culture, and national identity.

In 1707, the Union of the Crowns united Scotland and England, creating Great Britain while preserving Scottish traditions.
